Your Guide to
St. Margarets Bay

The best of our corner of the coast — most of it minutes from the front door, perfect for a short stay.

Welcome to The View! Everything here is close by — most spots a 5 to 15 minute drive along the bay. Tap Directions for the map or Call to check hours, since some local spots keep seasonal times. With just a night or two, we've kept this to our true favourites. Enjoy the bay.
This isn't an exhaustive list — just a handful of the spots we love most in our community. For more ideas during your stay, have a look at the Peggy's Cove Coastal Region Map, the Halifax 2026 Official Visitor Guide, and the Halifax Kids' Guide — hard copies are in the unit for you to browse.

What's On in July

Happening near the bay during your stay this month.

July 8 – 20 · Peggy's Cove area

Peggy's Cove Area Festival of the Arts

Two weeks of local art right along Peggy's Cove Road. Watch artists paint outdoors at "Paint Peggy's Cove" (July 11–13) and tour 40+ artist studios on the self-guided Studio Tour (July 18–20). Free family activities throughout.

July 7 – 12 · Halifax Waterfront

TD Halifax Jazz Festival

Six days of music with the main stage on the harbourfront. A great reason to pair an evening downtown with dinner by the water (about 35 min away).

July 15 – 18 · Halifax Waterfront

LatinFax

A free, family-friendly celebration of Latin music, food and culture on the Halifax waterfront.

Diner & lobster rolls•★ 4.4•Hacketts Cove · on Peggy's Cove Rd

Waterfront Pints & Plates

Right on the bay at Shining Waters Marina — minutes from the house.

Saltbox Brewing Co. — Shining Waters

Craft brewery & taproom•★ 4.4•Marina patio · Wed–Sun

Our closest waterfront pint — a Saltbox taproom set right above Shining Waters Marina at the head of the bay, with a big patio overlooking the water and regular live music on warm evenings. Local craft beer, ciders and hard iced teas, plus pub-style snacks. There's even a courtesy dock if you arrive by boat. A lovely, low-key spot to watch the sun go down over St. Margarets Bay.

→ Directions Call

Scotia by the Sea

Pub kitchen & Sunday brunch•Waterfront view•At the Saltbox taproom

The cozy kitchen inside the Saltbox taproom at Shining Waters, serving a full pub menu and a popular Sunday brunch — all with the same beautiful waterfront view. Pair a plate with a Saltbox pint on the marina patio. Hours follow the taproom, so a quick call ahead is wise, especially early or late in the season.

Beaches & Outdoors

Swim, paddle, and walk the shoreline — all on the bay.

Queensland Beach

Provincial park•★ 4.6•Change rooms & parking

One of the bay's best-loved beaches — clean fine sand, gentle waves, change rooms and washrooms. The water warms as summer goes on. Arrive early on sunny days, as parking fills and the beach shrinks at high tide.

→ Directions

Cleveland Beach

Provincial park•★ 4.4•Sandy + warm lagoon

A white-sand beach with a unique twist: a shallow tidal lagoon just steps from the ocean that warms in the sun — lovely for a float or a paddleboard. Sunsets here are stunning.

→ Directions

Micou's Island

Nature reserve•★ 4.8•Check the tides

A tranquil island you can walk to at low tide, with a short forest loop, little beaches, and tidal pools the kids will love. A real hidden gem — check the tide chart before you go and watch your timing.

→ Directions

Rum Runners Trail

Walk & bike path•Flat & scenic•Trailhead in Tantallon

A flat, scenic rail-trail that runs right along St. Margarets Bay — ideal for an easy walk or bike ride with water views the whole way. Rent wheels at the Bike & Bean and roll straight onto the path.

→ Directions

Hubbards Beach

Sandy beach•★ 4.6•~20 min · showers on site

A soft-sand, family-friendly beach at the head of the bay with changing rooms, showers, and fish & chips a short walk away. Worth the short drive on a warm day.

→ Directions

Family Fun with Kids

Big-splash days out for the little ones — a short drive inland.

Splashifax

Floating water park•★ 4.7•Hammonds Plains · ~25 min

Nova Scotia's first floating water park — a giant inflatable obstacle course of slides, climbing walls and trampolines (and the world's largest inflatable unicorn!) anchored on a private lake at Hatfield Farm, with lifeguards on hand. Sessions run about 100 minutes and include Coast Guard life jackets and petting-farm access. Book your session ahead online, and pack sunscreen and a towel. Open late June through summer.

→ Directions

Atlantic Splash Adventure

Water & amusement park•★ 3.8•Lucasville · ~25 min

A classic family amusement park about 20 minutes from Halifax with water slides, dry rides, go-karts and mini-golf — admission covers it all. Plenty of picnic tables, free parking and food on site. A full day's fun for kids of all ages on a warm day. Buying tickets online ahead of your visit can save a little and speeds up entry.

→ Directions Call

Hatfield Farm

Animal farm & wagon rides•★ 4.5•Hammonds Plains · ~25 min

A charming working farm where kids can pet and feed goats, donkeys, llamas, alpacas and horses, with horse-drawn wagon rides into the woods, an ice cream stand, food trucks and a gift shop. Lots of open space, ample parking and friendly cowboy-styled staff. (It's the same grounds as Splashifax — easy to pair the two.) A lovely, hands-on day for animal lovers.

→ Directions Call
✎ All three are seasonal and weather-dependent — check hours and book online before you set out. Micou's Island and the bay beaches (above) are great low-key kid days too.

Local Crafts & Makers

Handmade art and treasures along Peggy's Cove Road.

Every Tuesday · 2 – 6 PM

Tantallon Farmers Market

A friendly weekly market at the United Church on St. Margarets Bay Road — local produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts from area makers. A lovely way to meet the community if your stay falls on a Tuesday.

Peggy's Cove Glass

Hand-blown glass studio•★ 5.0•Seabright · Wed–Sun

A small working studio with stunning mouth-blown glass — nautical pieces, vases, ornaments and those mesmerizing patterned glass balls. You can often watch the artists at work. A one-of-a-kind keepsake.

→ Directions Call

Twelve Acre Hill Gallery

Painting studio & gallery•★ 5.0•Seabright

A quaint two-building gallery where artist Philip Hodges paints maritime landscapes — originals and prints, with the artist often on hand to chat. A lovely view from the property, too.

→ Directions Call

Cove Gallery

Art gallery & studio•★ 5.0•Seabright

A hidden gem just off the main road in Seabright — an artist-run gallery with an ever-changing collection of paintings, prints and carvings, plus handmade jewellery. Jim the artist is often there to greet you and share the story behind the work. Friendlier prices than the Cove, too.

→ Directions

Hags on the Hill

Artist co-op shop•★ 4.8•At Peggy's Cove

A collective shop run by 16 local artists right at Peggy's Cove — glass, jewellery, woodwork, leather and metal art. A welcome change from the usual souvenir shop, and you may meet the maker.

→ Directions Call
✎ Many area studios open extra hours during the Peggy's Cove Festival of the Arts (July 8–20) — a great time for the Studio Tour.

Close-By Sights

Iconic spots a short drive down the road.

Peggy's Cove

Iconic lighthouse•~25 min•Down your own road

Nova Scotia's most famous lighthouse on dramatic granite shores — a straight shot down Peggy's Cove Road from the house. Go early morning or near sunset to skip the tour buses. Mind the dark, wet rocks near the surf.

→ Directions

Swissair Flight 111 Memorial

Memorial & quiet walk•★ 4.8•Whalesback, near the Cove

A moving, peaceful memorial overlooking the ocean near Peggy's Cove, honouring the 229 lives lost in 1998. A short, easy walk from the parking lot leads to stunning views and a place for quiet reflection.

→ Directions

SS Atlantic Heritage Park

Heritage site & walk•★ 5.0•Terence Bay · Closed Sat

A peaceful seaside park and memorial in Terence Bay marking the 1873 wreck of the SS Atlantic. An easy boardwalk and gravel trail wind past benches, picnic tables and a small interpretation centre, with lovely ocean views — a quiet, picturesque stop and a worthwhile bit of local history.

→ Directions Call

Six by the Sea

A "village within the village" at Peggy's Cove — six restored heritage buildings.

Right at Peggy's Cove, six historic buildings have been lovingly restored into a little cluster of cafés, galleries and shops celebrating East Coast craft and history. It's an easy, walkable add-on to your lighthouse visit — wander between them, scan the QR codes outside each to learn the area's story, and grab a bite by the sea. Most are seasonal (roughly May–October) and open daily through summer. Below are the six.

Margaret's Café

Coffee, chowder & paninis•Light bites•Six by the Sea

A cozy café for coffee, seafood chowder, paninis and a sweet treat, with interiors full of Nova Scotian craftsmanship. Watch their socials for live Ceilidh music. A perfect warm-up or wind-down at the Cove.

→ Directions

Hunky Dory

Fries & soft-serve•Takeout window•Six by the Sea

A cheerful takeout window for gourmet fries, poutine and soft-serve ice cream, with picnic tables overlooking the sea. The name nods to the dory fishing boats of old — an easy, fun lunch with a view.

→ Directions

Holy Mackerel

Artisan shop & gifts•100+ local makers•Six by the Sea

A whimsical boutique stocked with work from over 100 Atlantic Canadian artisans — pottery, leather goods, candles, chocolates and one-of-a-kind keepsakes. The spot for a souvenir with real local soul (they ship, too).

→ Directions

Spindrift Gallery

Art gallery•Free to visit•Six by the Sea

A beautifully renovated gallery showing fine art and photography by Atlantic Canadian artists, with an upstairs loft for an artist-in-residence. Free to wander, with historical picture postcards you can mail right from the spot.

→ Directions

The Schoolhouse

Heritage site•Free, self-guided•Six by the Sea

Step back to 1839 in the village's tiny original schoolhouse — a free, self-guided peek at 19th-century seaside student life, where 32 children once squeezed into one room. A friendly attendant is often on hand with stories.

→ Directions

Arts & Culture Centre

Performances & programming•Six by the Sea

The sixth building rounds out the village as a space for intimate performances and cultural programming — part of the Peggy's Cove Centre for Arts & Culture. Check what's on during your visit for music or an event by the water.

→ Directions
✎ All six are seasonal — most open daily through summer, roughly 9 AM–6 PM. Pair with the lighthouse and Hags on the Hill (in Local Crafts) for a full Peggy's Cove afternoon.
